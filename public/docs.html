ClawPad/CLAW Addresses

CLAW Addresses

Vanity mint addresses for brand recognition

Every token launched through ClawPad has a mint address ending in CLAW. This is not random. We pre generate these vanity addresses using specialized algorithms, creating a pool of ready to use addresses for instant deployment.

Instant Brand Recognition

When you see a token address ending in CLAW, you know it was launched through ClawPad. This creates trust and brand consistency across all tokens on the platform.

How It Works

StepProcess
GenerationBackground workers continuously generate keypairs ending in CLAW
PoolAddresses are stored encrypted in our database, ready for use
ReservationWhen you confirm a blueprint, an address is reserved for your token
DeploymentThe reserved address becomes your token's mint address

Technical Details

Finding an address ending in CLAW requires approximately 450,000 attempts on average. We use the Solana keygen grind tool which runs multi threaded on our servers. Each address takes about 30 seconds to generate.

The private keys are encrypted using AES 256 GCM before storage. When a token is deployed, the key is decrypted in memory, used to sign the transaction, and immediately discarded. We never store unencrypted private keys.

Pool Status

ClawPad maintains a pool of at least 100 pre generated CLAW addresses. When the pool drops below a threshold, background workers automatically generate more. This ensures instant deployment without waiting for address generation.

ClawPad/Bonding Curve

Bonding Curve

Fair launch mechanics on pump.fun

All tokens launched through ClawPad use pump.fun's bonding curve mechanism. This is a mathematical formula that automatically sets the token price based on supply and demand. No presales, no whitelists, no insider allocation. Everyone buys from the same curve.

How Bonding Curves Work

Price Discovery

Token price increases as more people buy. Early buyers get lower prices.

Fair Access

No presales or allocations. Everyone has equal access to the curve.

Instant Liquidity

Buy or sell anytime. The curve always provides liquidity.

Graduation

When the curve fills, the token graduates to PumpSwap.

Token Distribution

Every pump.fun token has a total supply of 1 billion tokens. The bonding curve holds 800 million tokens available for purchase. The remaining 200 million are reserved for liquidity when the token graduates.

AllocationAmountPurpose
Bonding Curve800,000,000Available for public purchase
Liquidity Reserve200,000,000Paired with SOL at graduation
Total Supply1,000,000,000Fixed forever

Creator Fees

Token creators earn a small fee from every trade on the bonding curve. These fees accumulate in the token's wallet and are used by ClawPad for automatic buyback and burn operations.

ClawPad/Graduation

Graduation

From bonding curve to PumpSwap

When a token's bonding curve is filled, it "graduates" to PumpSwap, pump.fun's native decentralized exchange. This is a significant milestone that unlocks deeper liquidity and broader trading access.

What Happens at Graduation

  1. 1

    Curve Closes

    All 800 million tokens on the bonding curve have been purchased. No more buying or selling through the curve.

  2. 2

    Liquidity Migration

    The SOL collected and the 200 million reserved tokens are paired to create a liquidity pool on PumpSwap.

  3. 3

    LP Tokens Burned

    The liquidity pool tokens are permanently burned. This prevents rug pulls and ensures liquidity stays locked forever.

  4. 4

    Trading Continues

    The token is now tradeable on PumpSwap using standard AMM mechanics with 0.25% trading fees.

About PumpSwap

pump.fun tokens graduate directly to PumpSwap with zero migration fees. Trading stays within the pump.fun ecosystem.

Graduation Statistics

Less than 1% of tokens typically graduate. Most fail to generate enough interest to fill the bonding curve. ClawPad's automatic buyback mechanism helps increase graduation chances by maintaining consistent buy pressure.

ClawPad/Buyback & Burn

Buyback & Burn

Deflationary mechanics built in

ClawPad automatically executes buyback and burn operations for tokens launched on the platform. This creates consistent buy pressure and reduces circulating supply over time.

Why 60%

Using 60% of available fees per cycle means 40% accumulates for the next cycle. This creates a compounding effect where buybacks grow larger over time as the reserve builds up. It also ensures there is always fuel for the next buyback operation.

Transparent Operations

All buyback and burn transactions are recorded on chain and visible in the ClawPad dashboard. You can verify every operation through Solana explorers.

ClawPad/FAQ

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How much does it cost to launch a token?

Launching a token costs 0.025 SOL. This covers the pump.fun creation fee and Solana transaction costs. There are no additional fees from ClawPad.

How long does deployment take?

Once you deposit SOL, deployment typically completes within 30 seconds. You will see your token live on pump.fun almost immediately.

Can I use my own logo instead of AI generated ones?

Currently, ClawPad only supports AI generated logos. Custom logo upload is on our roadmap for a future release.

What happens if deployment fails?

If deployment fails for any reason, your SOL is automatically refunded to the wallet that sent the deposit. No manual action required.

Can I stop the buyback and burn operations?

No. Buyback and burn is a core feature of ClawPad tokens and cannot be disabled. This is by design to ensure consistent deflationary mechanics.

Why does my token address end in CLAW?

All tokens launched through ClawPad use pre generated vanity addresses ending in CLAW. This provides instant brand recognition and verifies the token was created through our platform.

Can I launch multiple tokens?

Yes. You can launch as many tokens as you want. Each launch requires a new 0.025 SOL deposit and goes through the full creation process.

What is the relationship between ClawPad and OpenClaw?

ClawPad is built on the OpenClaw AI agent framework. OpenClaw handles the AI capabilities like blueprint generation and natural language understanding. ClawPad adds the token launching, vanity addresses, and buyback mechanics on top.

Is ClawPad open source?

ClawPad uses the open source OpenClaw framework, but the ClawPad platform itself is not fully open source. The core infrastructure for vanity address generation and buyback operations is proprietary.
